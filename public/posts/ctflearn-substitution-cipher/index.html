Takeaways#

First, we counted the letter occurence and put candidate into them. This was very useful when filling the holes in words. Then we begin with short words to guess what it is. When we have multiple word candidates, we can always keep them and look for other and later come back.

When we have enough letters in the table, we can go for longer words. Which is faster but impossible when the table size is not enough.

For those least occured letters, they are not likely significant so we can leave them empty.

Lastly, we can always use tools for this job by searching substitution cipher.
